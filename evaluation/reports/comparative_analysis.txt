DQN achieves the highest success rate of 82.5%, outperforming all baselines.

Key takeaways:
  - DQN (learning-based): Learns optimal action selection from experience, adapting to ticket context beyond fixed rules.
  - Rule-Based (heuristic): Strong deterministic rules yield competitive performance but lack adaptability to edge cases.
  - RetrievalOnly (retrieval-based): Relies solely on similarity scores; effective when knowledge base coverage is high, but brittle otherwise.
  - Random: No strategy — serves as the lower-bound reference.

RL adds measurable value: the DQN agent learns when to suggest, clarify, or route based on the full state context, not just a single heuristic signal.
