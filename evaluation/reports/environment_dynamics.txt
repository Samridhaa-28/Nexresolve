NexResolve - Environment Dynamics (MDP) Specification
=====================================================

The environment is modeled as a semi-deterministic Markov Decision Process (MDP)
with rule-based transitions and data-driven signals.

Episode Definition:
- Start: Random ticket sampled from rl_ready_dataset.csv.
- End conditions:
  • Resolution success (resolution_success = 1)
  • SLA breach
  • ESCALATE action
  • Max interaction depth reached (~6 steps)

-----------------------------------------------------
STATE TRANSITIONS
-----------------------------------------------------

1. CLARIFY (ask_X)
------------------
- Reduces uncertainty and improves completeness
- May reduce knowledge gap indirectly
- Slight frustration increase (+0.03 to +0.05)
- Slight sentiment decrease
- May increase similarity (better retrieval after clarification)

2. ROUTE (route_X)
------------------
- Increases reassignment_count
- Slight SLA degradation
- No direct resolution effect
- Used for task delegation across teams

3. SUGGEST (suggest_X)
----------------------
Decision depends on retrieval confidence:

- If max_sim >= 0.6:
    → High probability of resolution_success = 1
    → Episode may terminate

- If max_sim < 0.6:
    → No resolution
    → Frustration increases (+0.1 to +0.2)
    → Possible reopen_count increase

- Tier influence:
    • Tier1: higher success probability
    • Tier2: lower success reliability

4. ESCALATE
-----------
- Immediate episode termination
- Used when system confidence is low or user frustration is high

-----------------------------------------------------
GLOBAL DYNAMICS
-----------------------------------------------------

- Turn count increases every step
- Interaction depth increases
- SLA remaining decreases gradually per step
- Frustration accumulates based on poor actions
- Knowledge gap remains unless resolved via clarify

-----------------------------------------------------
STOCHASTICITY
-----------------------------------------------------

- Environment includes controlled randomness:
  • Suggest success is probabilistic (based on similarity + tier)
  • Clarify may improve state variably

This improves generalization and prevents overfitting during DQN training.

-----------------------------------------------------
DESIGN GOAL
-----------------------------------------------------

- Encourage:
  • High-confidence suggestions
  • Minimal interaction steps
  • Correct routing decisions

- Discourage:
  • Unnecessary clarification
  • Premature escalation
  • Low-confidence suggestions
