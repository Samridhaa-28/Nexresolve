NexResolve Generator Evaluation Report
Overview
This report summarizes the evaluation of two response generation models:
FLAN-T5 and BART, tested on 100 samples from the NexResolve dataset.

Evaluation Metrics
The following metrics were used:
ROUGE-L (text overlap quality)
BERTScore (semantic similarity)
Structure Score (step-based formatting)
Grounding Score (solution alignment)
Composite Score (overall performance)

Results
FLAN-T5:

Average ROUGE-L Score : 0.0847
Average BERTScore (F1) : 0.8211
Average Structure Score : 0.26
Average Grounding Score : 0.0
Composite Score : 0.3886

BART:

Average ROUGE-L Score : 0.0023
Average BERTScore (F1) : 0.8098
Average Structure Score : 0.0
Average Grounding Score : 0.0
Composite Score : 0.2707

Analysis
FLAN-T5 significantly outperforms BART in ROUGE-L, indicating better lexical alignment with reference solutions.
BERTScore shows both models are semantically reasonable, but FLAN still leads.
Structure Score highlights a major difference: FLAN generates structured step-by-step responses, while BART fails to do so.
Grounding score is 0.0 for both models, suggesting that strict grounding metrics may need refinement.

Conclusion
FLAN-T5 is the superior model across all major evaluation metrics, especially in response structure and overall quality.

Final Decision:
Selected Generator Model: FLAN-T5
