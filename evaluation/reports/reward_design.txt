NexResolve - Reward Function Design Specification
================================================

Philosophy:
Reward is designed to balance:
- Fast resolution
- Correct decision making
- Customer satisfaction (low frustration)
- Minimal unnecessary actions

-----------------------------------------------------
BASE REWARDS
-----------------------------------------------------

Resolution Success:
+25 (strong terminal reward)

SLA Bonus:
+5 (if resolved before breach)

-----------------------------------------------------
PENALTIES
-----------------------------------------------------

Step Penalty:
-0.2 per step (encourages efficiency without overpowering reward)

Reassignment Penalty:
-2 (discourages unnecessary routing)

SLA Breach:
-10 (critical failure)

Frustration Increase:
-2 × Δ(frustration)
(dynamically penalizes poor user experience)

-----------------------------------------------------
STRATEGY-SPECIFIC SIGNALS
-----------------------------------------------------

CLARIFY:
- -1.5 if clarification not needed

SUGGEST:
- +3 if leads to resolution
- +2 bonus for Tier1 cases
- -0.5 penalty for Tier2 cases
- -1.5 if suggestion fails
- -1.5 if similarity < 0.4 (bad suggestion context)

ESCALATE:
- -5 if done prematurely (low frustration)

-----------------------------------------------------
REWARD CHARACTERISTICS
-----------------------------------------------------

- Success trajectories produce significantly higher reward than failures
- Failures accumulate penalties over multiple steps
- Reward distribution:
    • Successful cases: higher (less negative or positive)
    • Failed cases: strongly negative

-----------------------------------------------------
CLIPPING
-----------------------------------------------------

Rewards are clipped to:
[-15, 40]

This ensures:
- Stability during DQN training
- Preservation of strong success signal

-----------------------------------------------------
VALIDATION (EMPIRICAL)
-----------------------------------------------------

From replay buffer analysis:

- Success reward >> Failure reward
- Suggest actions dominate successful trajectories
- Clear separation exists for learning

This confirms reward is suitable for DQN.
