INTERPRETATION

1. SIMILARITY DISTRIBUTION
   The average similarity score (~0.78) indicates that the retrieval system is consistently finding reasonably close matches for most queries. The presence of values close to 1.0 suggests either exact matches or near-duplicate issues in the knowledge base. The relatively high standard deviation (~0.19) confirms that the system produces a wide spread of similarity scores, which is important for downstream decision-making. This variation enables the RL agent to distinguish between strong, weak, and uncertain retrieval scenarios.

---

2. BUCKET ANALYSIS
   The bucketed results clearly show a strong relationship between similarity and resolution success:

* Low and mid_low buckets (similarity < 0.5) have zero success rates, indicating that weak retrievals do not lead to successful resolutions.
* The mid_high bucket (0.5–0.7) shows negligible success (~0.004), suggesting that moderate similarity is still insufficient for reliable suggestion.
* The high similarity bucket (> 0.7) shows a sharp increase in success (~0.57), confirming that strong retrieval is a key driver of resolution.

This validates the use of similarity thresholds in decision-making and strongly supports prioritizing suggestion actions only when similarity is high.

---

3. KNOWLEDGE GAP ANALYSIS
   The knowledge gap signal shows perfect separation:

* gap = 1 → 0% success
* gap = 0 → ~40.8% success

This demonstrates that the knowledge_gap_flag is a highly reliable indicator of whether the system has sufficient prior knowledge to solve the issue. When a gap is detected, suggestions are ineffective, reinforcing the need to route, clarify, or escalate instead. This makes knowledge_gap_flag a critical feature for action masking and policy learning.

---

4. HIGH CONFIDENCE ANALYSIS
   High-confidence retrievals (max_sim ≥ 0.85) show extremely strong predictive power:

* Success rate: ~84.2%
* Non-high-confidence success: ~0.8%

This indicates that when the system is highly confident, suggestions are almost always correct. Therefore, high confidence should directly drive aggressive suggestion behavior in the RL policy. This also validates the chosen high-confidence threshold and supports using it as a trigger for prioritizing suggestion actions.

---

5. INTENT MATCH RATE
   The retrieval correctness proxy (~81.8%) indicates that the system retrieves relevant issues in the majority of cases. This is a strong baseline for a semantic retrieval system without explicit supervised ranking. It confirms that the embedding model and FAISS index are functioning effectively and that retrieved candidates are meaningful for downstream use.

---

6. CORRELATION ANALYSIS

* Similarity vs Success (0.80):
  A very strong positive correlation, indicating that higher similarity directly leads to higher resolution success. This validates similarity as one of the most important features in the RL state.

* Similarity vs Reopen (0.078):
  Weak correlation, suggesting that similarity alone does not strongly predict reopen behavior. Reopen is likely influenced by other factors such as incomplete information or poor routing.

* Knowledge Gap vs Success (-0.286):
  Negative correlation confirms that knowledge gaps reduce the likelihood of successful resolution. This reinforces the importance of avoiding suggestion actions when a gap is detected.

---

7. OVERALL CONCLUSION
   The evaluation demonstrates that RAG-derived features—especially max_sim, knowledge_gap_flag, and high-confidence indicators—are highly informative and strongly aligned with real outcomes. These features provide a reliable foundation for RL decision-making, enabling the agent to:

* Prefer suggestion when similarity is high
* Avoid suggestion when a knowledge gap exists
* Adjust behavior based on confidence and retrieval strength
